READING RESPONSE

In response to 'My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge. What could yours be?' by Laurel Schwulst

Thanks to the easy access through servers, we are able to browse through various websites and present ourselves to almost anyone in the world. Website not only is a handy tool for us to represent our identity as artists and designers, its values also lie in its openness, sharing culture as well as the infinite possibilities we are able to create through the web. As described in Laurel's article, a website could be seen as a growing plant, a house next to a river and one of a handful of thrown rocks. I wonder how one can make a website that reflects such idea that website could have continuituous growth, influence and unlimited results.

The design of this website is intended to provide a simulation of open source. Through offering a gridded template for anyone to input texts and create "their own" visual and literary, the website ideally will update itself with the creations people make. As Schwulst mentioned, there is no state of “completeness” to a website. The website sets up a playful sharing culture and newness to audience everytime they return to the website. Although people are able to navigate within and create through the website, they are limited by a predefined structure that is set up by me.

Through making this website, I question how a website could grow like a plant? What does that actually mean? Does that mean an AI could facilitate the growth of the seed we plant for our website? And another question: could there by a truly open source? If there is, what does that mean? Is it a source where anyone who has access to the website could manipulate its infrastucture, design and/or its parameter and usage? At the end, I wonder if we could even break out of the limitations of "website". Rather than relying on our web pages and visuals, perhaps there could be other ways of obtaining information that entirely replace the culture of "web browsing" in the near future.
